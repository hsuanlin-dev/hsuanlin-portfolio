## Overview

I was the sole contributor for the visual layer of the Indigenous Carbon Sink platform, an ongoing ESG initiative connecting corporate carbon-offset goals with indigenous tribal land conservation. National Taipei University served as the bridge between the platform and a group of 20+ participating companies, and the site was presented at the Legislative Yuan early in the project.

## Challenge

The platform needed to work reliably in remote indigenous mountain communities, where network conditions are often slow and unstable. I also had to reconcile feedback from more than 20 companies — each with their own opinions — into one coherent design, all while owning the visual identity and content end to end, with no separate design or copywriting team to lean on.

## What I Did

- Built an initial version of the platform, then revised it based on feedback from discussions with the 20+ participating companies
- Designed the platform's visual identity, including the core symbol used across the brand, and wrote all of the site's narrative content and copy
- Built the frontend with Bootstrap and vanilla JS instead of a heavier modern framework, to keep things fast and reliable on the limited network conditions common in remote indigenous areas
- Implemented a custom SCSS architecture with a defined color system and responsive layout

## Outcome

Shaped by that round of stakeholder feedback, the platform was presented at the Legislative Yuan early on, and continues to serve as an ongoing promotional tool for National Taipei University's ESG carbon-offset initiative with indigenous communities.
